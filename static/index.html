Getting started
Learn in three steps
No experience needed — each step builds on the last.
01

Choose a market & timeframe

Pick any ticker — stocks, ETFs, or crypto — then select a timeframe. Watching how the same indicator behaves on a 5-minute chart versus a weekly chart is one of the most valuable lessons here.

02

Adjust the parameters & observe

Use the sliders to change RSI lookback periods, MACD fast/slow settings, Bollinger Band width, and EMA lengths. The goal isn't to find the "right" settings — it's to understand what each parameter controls and why it matters.

03

Run a backtest & reflect

Apply your configuration to historical data and review the results — win rate, drawdown, trade frequency. Use this not as a prediction of future performance, but as a window into how a particular rule set has behaved in the past.
