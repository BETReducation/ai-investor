WHY REBALANCING WORKS
01
SYSTEMATIC PROFIT-TAKING
When an asset class rises sharply, it takes up more than its 20% share. Rebalancing forces you to sell it at elevated prices and rotate into underperformers — mechanically implementing "sell high, buy low" without needing to predict the market. Try the Crypto Bull Run scenario, then rebalance, then simulate a crash.
02
UNCORRELATED ASSET CLASSES
Crypto, stocks, property, metals and bonds rarely crash simultaneously. When stocks collapse, bonds and gold often rise. A five-way split means a 70% crash in one class only destroys 14% of a balanced portfolio — versus 70% of a concentrated one. Try the Stock Market Crash scenario to see this in action.
03
COMPOUNDING THE REBALANCE
The real power comes from repeating this over many cycles. Apply Crypto Bull Run → Rebalance → simulate a crash. The balanced portfolio locked in gains at the top. The concentrated investor rides it all the way back down. Over decades, this compounding effect of disciplined rebalancing can significantly outperform emotional concentration.
